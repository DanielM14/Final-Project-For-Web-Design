There are many great athletes who made them selves great by hard work and dedication. These are the top five NFL players of all time with some of their stats along with pictures.

RB Jim Brown -- Clevelabd Browns, 1957-1965

Forty years after his shocking retirement at the peak of his career, and now only seventh on the all-time rushing list, Brown still ranks as the standard against which all other running backs are measured.

Rushing Yards 12,312
Average Yards 5.2 (Best amoung the top 20 all-time rushers)

WR Jerry Rice -- 49ers, Raiders, Seahawks, 1984-2004

Rice's work ethic was, and still is at 41, unparalleled and that's a major reason why he's one of the greatest players in the history of the NFL. He not only made big catches in the games that didn't matter, he also made them when the games mattered.

Touchdowns 205
Receptions 1,519
Receiving Yards 22,466

QB Joe Montana -- 49ers, Chief, 1979-1994

Montana might not have been the prototypical quarterback, but he found ways to win and was arguably the best fourth-quarter quarterback in the history of the game. If I had to start a team with a proven winner, it would probably start with Montana.

Att 5,391
Comp 3,409
Pct 63.2
Pass Yards 40,551
Touchdowns 273

RB Walter Payton -- Bears, 1975-1987

In 13 seasons, many of them played with a subpar Bears team, the man known as "Sweetness" missed just one start. What he missed even more rarely was any kind of crease through which he could slither.

Rushing Yards 16,726
Touchdowns 110
Receiving Yards 4,538

LB Lawreence Taylor -- Giants, 1981-1993

He recorded 132.5 career sacks in 13 seasons with the New York Giants - a quaint number by today's standards set by Bruce Smith and Reggie White - but don't let the numbers fool you. Lawrence Taylor revolutionized the art of sacking the quarterback.

Sacks 132.5
Int 9
